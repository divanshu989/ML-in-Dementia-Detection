Understanding Dementia

Dementia is a complex neurological condition that affects millions worldwide. It's not a specific disease, but a syndrome characterized by progressive cognitive decline.

Key Aspects:

  • Memory loss beyond normal aging
  • Challenges in communication
  • Impaired reasoning and judgment
  • Changes in personality and behavior

Global Impact:

Over 55 million people worldwide live with dementia, with nearly 10 million new cases every year.

Support Strategies

Developing effective support strategies can significantly improve quality of life for individuals living with dementia.

  • • Establish consistent routines
  • • Create a safe living environment
  • • Encourage social interaction
  • • Use memory aids and reminders

Early Detection

Recognizing early signs and seeking professional help can lead to better management and support.

Warning Signs:

  • • Memory loss disrupting daily life
  • • Difficulty planning or solving problems
  • • Challenges completing familiar tasks
  • • Confusion with time or place
